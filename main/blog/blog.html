Schedule: Makers VS Managers - April 7, 2018

The art of a person's schedule. The well known calendar invite. Some (let's refer to this group as "Managers") live by this tool and cannot function properly without checking it every morning to plan the upcoming day. However, for others (let's refer to these people as "Makers") see this tool as more of a creative killer, a buzzkill, a wet blanket (I think you get my point) that just becomes a part of life that you learn to live with because society deems it "normal". Growing up in a very "Always show up 5 minutes early" type environment, it was definitely instilled in me at a very young age that it is rude to show up late for an appointment/meeting/commitment. Humans as a whole, respect the value of time and do our best to manage it, control it, and try to optimize it throughout our lives. The issue that I have run into with this type of mindset is that creation/creativity does not live on a timed schedule. Now, of course there are ways to set aside time for creative thoughts and process; however, when you get "real" with yourself, sometimes creativity just strikes during your morning shower, or in the middle of a meeting, or maybe right before you fall asleep. My inital instinct when this little beam of brilliance hits me is to write it down, sketch it up, or code that motha until the cows come home. Unfortunately, herein lies the issue ... I cannot just get up and leave a meeting to go indulge on a little creative snack. I find if my entire day is following a "Managers" schedule that very little creative work gets done that day. Usually what occurs is a lot of planning, and discussing, and perhaps some problem generation gets done, but usually very little out of the box thinking occurs. On the flip side, if I block out maybe the first four hours of my morning, just to hold from being scooped up by the meeting troll ... then more times than not, something cool and new is generated or brought into existence to play a role (maybe) in this wonderful world called Design.

Land Beneath the Ice - June 26, 2017

Greetings from Whittier, AK! This is James currently on the keys, about to dive into some incredible memories from yesterday’s unbelievable trip to the “Little Fishing Town Beneath the Ice”. Maegan and I set off for this adventure at 4:30a in the morning (I would usually say in the early dark parts of the morning, but it is currently sunny basically all day, so actually we had our shades on and sunblock ready to go). Our 3 hour drive was nothing less than incredible as we dipped, dove, climbed and crawled around mountains and valleys through the back roads of this beautiful place called the “Last Frontier”. Arriving a bit early for our 26 Glacier Cruise, Maegan and I decided to get lost in the town a bit and figure out where we would be staying that night. Immediately after walking down the road a few blocks, you could tell that we were no longer in Kansas (so to speak). This was a very old fishing town, that had seen the hardship of several cold/brutal winters over the years. I instantly fell in love with the multitude of creative ideas that started rushing through my mind. How gnarly it would be to spend a month or two here, just writing and drinking … coffee (wink) as life goes on back in the lower 48. Maegan could perform therapy on all the sailors and fisherman, and I could write and fish … what more do you need! After a few hours, we had located a hostel for us to crash in that night, filled our bellies with some delicious reindeer breakfast sandwiches, and were ready for the cruise of a lifetime. Captain Speedy, tore out of the harbor on a mission. We were going to be on a tight deadline to literally see 26 glaciers in less than 5 hours. I will give him some props though, we hit them all and saw some unbelievable wildlife along the way. Maegan was able to capture some unreal snaps of a beautiful humpback whale, sea lion, otters, tons of glaciers, a few waterfalls, and her “Ballin Little Bro” diving into the ocean (blink). Capt Speedster brought us back to the harbor right on time like a pro. No words (although I always try) could really do justice to what we just experienced. The Dougherty Duo will be setting off for Anchorage today as we putz around Whitter for a few more hours looking for new adventures. You never know what He may bring as I sit gazing out my hostel window overlooking the Pacific Ocean, a magnificent little hummingbird has been buzzing around me as a blessing from my late great mother no doubt. Life is something special, my friends. Nothing more to do, but enjoy the ride.

Honduras Day 6 - October 22, 2015

Day 6: Until Next Time. The end has finally arrived, unfortunately. Day 6 was by far the hardest emotionally just knowing that we would be traveling to the airport the next morning, rather than back on the bus to continue our service journey. It was such a great day though as a whole from beginning to end. Our teams were finally in the same areas working together as one unit. Medical saw over 600 patients this week which is really incredible for a team smaller than 10. This morning we finished the mission at a girls orphanage to make sure they all had physicals from the medical staff and had prayer for anything going on in their lives. I had the honor of praying over eight girls with the Medical Minister. His words and passion for the Lord is unlike any other. I can see his light shining through as soon as he walks in a door. I pray that he will continue to serve and pray for others even after returning to Pittsburgh. Later in the morning we decided to play kickball, basketball, futbol, and dance (they loved the cupid shuffle). There was no lesson or plan we just wanted to laugh and play like kids are supposed to do at that age. There was no fighting, no arguing, just good old fashion fun. Reflecting back on these past 6 days kept reminding me that none of this would have been possible without Head Honcho and Seis. Of course someone else could have led this trip and I’m sure it would have went just fine, but this was not a “fine” trip. This was a trip for the ages that has sparked a fire in the Crochet Queen and me to continue on these acts of services for many years to come. Head Honcho and Seis did an unbelievable job leading our Missions team and I would like to thank them publically for all that God has done in their lives and pray that he will continue to work wonders for these two. Our night ended with a farewell dinner for our team, our coordinators, the security, our amazing translators, and our awesome driver/guide. Everyone looked great all cleaned up, ready for a good meal and even better conversation. I wanted the night to continue for hours, since I knew the time for goodbyes had arrived. I have never been a fan of goodbyes, I try to avoid them by sneaking out of parties early, sending texts rather than meeting in person, or just ignoring them altogether; however, these were inevitable. I will pray for out extended team, and hope to see many of them again some day. A verse from one of the songs we sang during our nighttime devotionals is, “Your love is deeper than my view of grace, higher than this worldly place, longer than this road I’ve traveled, wider than the gap you’ve filled.” We never know what God has in store for us next, whether it be a missions trip, a new calling, an old friendship coming back to life, we cannot predict the future. All we can do in this life is to love on those who stroll into our road traveled, and have faith that good will always overcome evil.

Honduras Day 5 - October 21, 2015

Day 5: A Day I Will Never Forget. Today was the Missions Team’s last day at the Family Hope Center and the Sinai School. Our morning kicked off with an extremely powerful prayer session with all the mothers broken into smaller groups to share with our team what was on their hearts. You could literally feel the positivity working around our circles within the Spanish and English prayers. The Holy Spirit was undeniable this morning! It just baffles me how strong these women are who are single parents, raising their young ones, with very low opportunity for employment; however, the majority of their prayers were for others. You never heard them ask for more money or a nicer home, instead they asked for blessings over their children and parents, better opportunities for their sisters and brothers, and prayers for the government to help steer this country back in the right direction. Our team decided to end our time with them learning how to make headbands out of cut up shirts. The women mastered this technique instantly and began using the materials to improvise our lesson plan. They began braiding beautiful patterns making headbands, bracelets, and belts to give as gifts or wear around town. We ended our time together singing a song in Spanish led by one woman who in my opinion would make it onto The Voice hands down. Although we could not understand the lyrics, we could feel the meaning and joy from the ladies clapping and smiling. I woke up knowing that we would be saying goodbye to the little ones later in the day, but was not expecting how attached I had gotten to a few that reminded me of myself at a young age. When you Google San Pedro Sula, your initial thoughts are the crime, gangs, poverty, corruption, but these children are truly no different than you and I at their core. They have the basic human needs, they love sports, puzzles, coloring, singing, dancing, jump roping, making arts and crafts, and piggy back rides (at one point I think the kids thought that Head Honcho and I were their personal horses, yelling VAMOS as we ran by one another tired, but happy to be exactly where we were). The afternoon went extremely smooth even with the downpour causing us to change up the game plan. Sadly, our time with these kids had come to an end. After several high-fives and hugs, our new amigos were out the door and back to life as they knew it. I pray that our time together will be remembered when tough roads present themselves to these young souls. I pray that they will always remember that Jesus es Super Fuerte, and that He will never leave their sides in times of need. I will never forget this community for the rest of my days. I will pray for them always.

Honduras Day 4 - October 20, 2015

Day 4: Today the Son was shining bright! This morning we sent our medical team to the clinic, which I will discuss in much further detail later. However, now we were off to the family hope center to recap day two with the Crochet Queen and the mother’s of Las Brisas. Today’s topic was more private, thus, the men were not invited. Instead we enjoyed legos in a side room with the women’s children and I will tap out on this blog, if you will, while CQ updates you on the morning with the women. Hola!!! Today was another perfect day. The women were so open and so perceptive to the discussion. I will respect their confidentiality and keep the meat of our discussion private. I had special help from Seis, the Traveling Heart, and Wise Scholar as well as a team of translators. One thing I found striking was the similarities between the culture in Honduras and in the States. The topic was a very broad understanding of health, human growth and development, and ways to protect ourselves. Common issues the women discussed, our ministry team agreed were similar issues we notice in our own interpersonal circles. After the discussion, we moved on to arts and crafts! I was not born a Queen of Crocheting. My Grandmother spent many years teaching me. Today, I got to give back and share this trade with 40 plus women. Nicole and Carol helped translate what I was doing step by step and the women picked it up faster than anyone I had ever seen! I also got to educate my own team members which was such an awesome bonding experience. Seis and Wise Scholar will be making scarves and blankets for the holidays, take your orders now! The morning flew right before my eyes. I cannot wait to continue the lesson tomorrow. The Traveling Heart also led a craft, one that we all got instant gratification from: awesome headbands made from tying t-shirts together. Seriously, check them out, they’re sweet! She mastered these in less than 10 minutes. Tomorrow, we will also spend time showing the women this trade. Headbands are a popular accessory in San Pedro Sula, as the weather is hot. I’ve sweat in places I didn’t even know existed, TMI? So having a band to collect the sweat is very useful. Our hope is the women can reproduce headbands on their own and make an income from selling them in their community. CQ out! Our ministry team headed back to regroup at the clinic with the medical team to enjoy lunch together and hear about one another’s day, I had the chance to catch up with Big Cheese and his team of talented medical professionals. To my surprise this day was a bit different then the previous two. Our team did not see many walking well during their hours unfortunately. One adult woman who weighed around 75 pounds was having a serious issue with having her body basically eat itself from trying to breastfeed her new born child. The saddest part is that she is just trying to provide nutrients for her niño; however, the effect is that it is actually damaging her physically. Another patient was a young man (younger than me) who had a scar from his left shoulder, a crossed his throat, up his head from a machete battle. Apparently it is the norm for boys to have a machete by the age of 12 or 13. If that alone does not make your jaw drop, the amount of machete fights that occur in Honduras might make that jaw hit the floor. A patient that I actually got the chance to see at the clinic was an elderly woman that had wounds all over her feet. Big Cheese later told me that her feet were basically destroying themselves from the severe case of untreated diabetes. In the states this women would have been hospitalized for at least two weeks, no questions asked. However, since Honduras is not fortunate enough to have these resources, her wounds were cleaned and bandages and she was on her way after about 20 minutes. I ask all you readers out there to continue praying not only for our incredible teams and the work that we are doing down here, but for all the patients and children that will not be leaving to return to a country where there is an Urgent Care on every corner and a bathroom that has working plumbing.

Honduras Day 3 - October 19, 2015

Day 3: Jesus es Super Fuerte. “All I need is you Lord is you Lord. Lord all I need is You!” As I sit listening to these lyrics from Hillsong, reflecting on the last thirteen hours of day tres, I cannot stop thinking about how blessed I am to have been a part of this trip/this group. Our team is led by unbelievable, strong, wise, LOVING individuals that when put together in SPS move mountains to make a difference. Our day began meeting with a large group of single mothers whose children have been in our prayers for sometime now. The women met us at the Hope Center where you could tell as they walked in the door, they did not know what to expect over the next three-hour session. To say these women have had a hard life would be like saying that Pittsburgh only kind of likes the Steelers (understatements of the century). I had been praying about what was about to occur next the majority of the previous night, as well as, the morning during our small group devotional. My wife, let’s refer to her as The Crochet Queen in this blog, has a background in mental health counseling. Her goal during today’s session was to not only educate these wonderful mothers about wellness and how we can practice a healthier lifestyle, but to also listen to actual concerns and issues that these ladies were currently going through in their everyday lives. Watching my wife in her element is something that every husband should have the privilege of doing numerous times throughout a marriage. Women who were reluctant at first to open up, started sharing stresses in their lives about relationships, worries, pains that caused them unwanted and really speaking honestly unneeded anxiety in their lives. Our ministry team worked with these mothers to teach them coping mechanisms (meditation & stress ball creation) to help relieve the stress in a healthy way, which can be preformed no matter where they are or what time of the day. Meditation seemed to be a huge hit with not only the mothers, but also our ministry team. Who knew that something so easy as closing your eyes and picturing a series of images could release so much tension. The stress balls were more of a Do It Youself tutorial, where our team took balloons and filled them with flour to form a pretty legitimate stress reliever. However, never having actually practiced this prior to the demonstration the students quickly became the teachers, showing us a thing or two about how to handle flour without dumping the majority on the pavement. The session concluded with prayer requests and hands being layed on those in need. The power of prayer never seizes to amaze me, even with the language barrier your heart is filled with such a passion for these people in this community just trying to protect and give their children a life that is safe and fruitful. The second part of our day was focused around Vacation Bible School or VBS. Children poured into the Clinic’s courtyard bull rushing me like I was the last Power Chicken in all of SPS. Quickly before I knew it, I had one minion climbing on my back, two bigger minions hanging from my arms, and a new pair of shoes that weighed about fifty pounds each. These kids had the ability to put a smile on your face no matter what any of us had going on back in the states. One of our leaders, let’s call her Seis, started playing music that caught all the kids attention immediately. The name of the song was Super Fuerte, which in English translates to Super Strong. Children were jumping up and down, clapping their hands, playing air guitar, and some even rushed the stage, probably to try and get Seis’s autograph. The Lord was truly working wonders yesterday during VBS, our medical team said that you could feel the energy throughout the entire clinic. We finished the day off by teaching the children a lesson about conviction. The kids then had the option to make arts and crafts, color, create puzzles, play futbol in the courtyard, or build with legos. Out of those options the coloring room was the stations with the most chaos. Who knew that Honduran children loved coloring books more than futbol?! Our drive back to the hotel was very different then previous days. Our clothes were dirty, our body’s were exhausted, but our smiles and stories from the day were all that we focused on.

Honduras Day 2 - October 18, 2015

Day 2: The Gifts That Keep On Giving. Greetings blog followers! Day 2 in SPS was a powerful day to say the least. Our North Way crew started our morning off picking up 7 incredible/essential translators to help with the language barrier that we face this week. Our bus of 20+ members made our way to a popular Honduran clinic where we dropped off supplies for our amazing medical team to prep for the 120 patients they were about to see. Our medical leader, Big Cheese, said that even on a busy day 120 is more than twice the amount of patients he would ever see in one day back in the states with our size medical group. While the medical team was laying healing hands on those in need, the ministry team was off at Iglesia del Nazareno sitting patiently in an empty church eagerly awaiting our first Honduran church service to begin. The sign directly behind the stage read, “Hacer Discipulos Semejantes a Cristo en las Naciones”, which in English loosely translates to Go therefore and make disciples of all nations. As I sat and thought about what making disciples really means to me as a Christian, my thought process was broken by a whirlwind of little feet stampeding towards the big empty room where we all sat. Thinking back on the moment now, the best way to describe it would be a blur of Honduran children rushing towards us with ear-to-ear smiles. To call these kids shy could not be farther from the truth. After settling, we began passing the peace, as we would say in America. However, in Honduras the peace is literally passed to everyone in the room. Almost like a small party that happens pre-sermon. It was such a welcoming and wonderful feeling to have the majority of the church come and greet you just for being a part of their community on such a special Sunday. Pastor Victor gave an incredible sermon that was translated by our wonderful new friend, Carol. We learned about the human nature of always asking for things from God without always obeying Him in how we live our lives. If we ask our Father for things that are set on our hearts, we need to be prepared to follow in Christ’s footsteps as He has asked for all of His children. The service was completed by several worship songs and then a very powerful praying session all done in Spanish. Although I really could not understand the majority of either, I could feel the Spirit working throughout the entire room in one way or another. Our team was truly blessed to witness such a beautiful event. As we made our way to the Buckner Center, many of us were not expecting what was about to occur next. Our team began setting up shoes that had been donated to Buckner for this trip. About 50 shoes of various sizes and colors were now organized to be distributed to all those in need. A row of chairs were lined up for our soon to be happy customers. One by one a child entered with old dirty shoes, sat on the chair (little legs not long enough to touch the ground), and immediately a smile appeared as a new pair of shoes were brought over. Our team took pride in taking off the old pair, washing away the dirt with a fresh pair of socks, and fitting a brand new pair of sneakers onto a foot that had new roads and paths just waiting to be discovered in their young little lives. Forty-six shoes were given away by the end of the afternoon, and four were being switched out for a different size that will be given away tomorrow. I have bought a lot of sneakers in my day; however, not a single pair had made me as happy as giving a pair to children in need today. On our way back to the hotel, we needed to make a few pit stops to stock up on some additional supplies. As the majority of the team waited in the bus, our driver and coffee connoisseur began giving us a bit of a history lesson on Honduras to pass the time. One question led to another, and before we knew it somewhere between coffee being made from milk and espresso beans dipped in cinnamon sugar, he opened up about being a single father raising a 16-year-old son and 14-year-old daughter while working full-time to provide for his family. My heart felt for this strong Christian man who had been through so much in his life, but still shined the light of Christ on all those around him. Please continue to keep this team and all those we have encountered/will be encountering this week in your prayers. Each day continues to bring us closer to our Lord and one another as we serve.

Luis has come up in conversation several times in the past few months while prepping for this journey. He is basically our “go-to” guy for all things Honduran. The man definitely lives up to all the hype. He met us in the airport, shortly after arriving, and gained my trust immediately when one of our bags was flagged for something “suspicious”. My guess was all the balloons and/or crafts for our Bible Study activities with the kids this week. Nevertheless, Luis was on top of this from square one and everything was handled and back to normal within minutes. Having now reached our destination (trust me the 100% humidity is hard to miss walking out of the airport doors) my eyes were immediately open to the culture change when we had to keep a watch on our bags until they made their way from our hands to a helper who would then place them into our van. This was to avoid any runaway luggage that would never return. Our agenda for the rest of Day 1 was to go over a few basic rules that were non-negotiable for the week, grab some food from my now favorite fast food restaurant of all time (Power Chicken!!), attend our first Church service of the trip, and finish the night off with a team dinner and breakdown. Unfortunately the Church service was cancelled due to flooding from all the rain that had occurred during the day. Hopefully this will not hinder our plans for the upcoming week since the forecast for the next several days is rain, rain, and more rain. All in all though Day 1 was a great success, bringing our team closer together as a whole and closer to God as followers. We thank Him for this wonderful opportunity, and are so blessed to be able to spend this next week putting Him above all.

Honduras Day 0 (Packing Day) - October 10, 2015

Packing Day! This morning the 2015 fall Honduras Team met one last time to pack all the supplies for next week’s trip. It is hard to believe we are only one week away from a trip we have all been preparing for the last few months. This morning we each brought a suitcase to fill full of supplies. In each suitcase we packed donated medicine, donated clothes, hygiene products, dictionaries, calculators, donated medicine, school supplies, bible study supplies, arts and crafts, toys, and more donated medicine. Did I mention the donated medicine?! Our group is beyond thankful for all the donations and prayers we have received in preparation for this trip. From clothes to medicine to helping fund each and every one of our trips; our gratitude is immeasurable. Please continue to keep us in your thoughts and prayers as we continue to prepare physically, emotionally and spiritually. So what is next? We will meet at the airport October 17th at 4:30 in the morning to begin our adventure to San Pedro Sula. We will be there for a full week helping any way we can (don’t worry we will update this blog at least once maybe twice a day to fill you in on all of our many adventures!) We are all experiencing many emotions but the main theme is excitement. We have been well prepared by our leaders and are ready to put our hands to work.

Obsession? More Like a Passion - February 2, 2014

The realization that I have mild OCD has become very apparent to me these past 6 months. Maegan points it out sometimes, but I have always known that it twas there. I have this weird thing about keeping my electronics clean. I know that the reasoning behind this is that electronics fascinate me to the highest degree. There is just something about a clean screen on a brand new piece of hardware that has the latest and fastest software uploaded on it that makes me light up. The issue with this is that everything gets dim, dusty, and smudged after time and use. I am consciously trying to change my weird ways of keeping my laptop screen clean. The whole point of these awesome inventions is to serve us, not the other way around. I will keep my readers (1) posted on this issue, but rest easy people (1) I do not lose sleep over this weird little quirk of mine. “All things new and clean, have no story nor past within their journey” — Yours truly
